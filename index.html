Hi, I'm Stacy. Bad UX makes me irrationally angry, and I can't unsee it. So I'm becoming an interaction designer and usability tester, because apparently this is a whole career, and I'd rather get paid to be annoyed than keep doing it for free.

My method? Equal parts curious, stupid, and brave. Push the red button, break things apart, put them back together. Grew up on innovation and duct tape. I learn by doing, I'm rooted in logic, and I fly by gut. Don't give me a long presentation on the problem. Hand me the broken thing, say "this doesn't make sense," and let me get consumed by the puzzle of figuring it out.

I'm also the one who shows up with the red pen. I'll find the mistakes, help you fix them, and tell you when your fly is down. Respectfully.

Toolkit: Figma, usability testing, accessibility audits, heuristic evaluation, WCAG, NN/g, Apple HIG, Material, Buffy the Vampire Slayer trivia.
